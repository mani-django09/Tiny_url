7. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

We and our third-party service providers may use cookies, web beacons, and other tracking technologies to track information about your use of our Service. We may combine this information with other personal information we collect from you.

Cookies

Cookies are small data files stored on your hard drive or in device memory that help us improve our Service and your experience, see which areas and features of our Service are popular, and count visits. For more information about cookies and how to disable them, please see your browser's help section.

Web Beacons

We may use web beacons (also known as "pixel tags" or "clear GIFs") on our Service. Web beacons are tiny graphic images with a unique identifier, similar in function to cookies, that are used to track the online movements of web users. In contrast to cookies, which are stored on your computer's hard drive, web beacons are embedded invisibly on web pages.

Analytics

We use third-party analytics services, such as Google Analytics, to help understand your usage of our Service. These services collect information sent by your device or our Service, including the web pages you visit, add-ons, and other information that assists us in improving the Service. This information may be used to compile reports and to help us improve the Service.

Do Not Track

Some browsers offer a "Do Not Track" feature, which signals to websites that you do not want to have your online activities tracked. We currently do not respond to "Do Not Track" signals because there is no standard for how online services should respond to such signals. If we do so in the future, we will provide updated information about our Do Not Track practices in this Privacy Policy.
