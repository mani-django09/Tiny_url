EXPERT ADVICE

QR Code Best Practices

Follow these tips to maximize the effectiveness of your QR codes

Size Matters

Make sure your QR code is large enough to be easily scanned. As a general rule, the minimum size should be 2 x 2 cm (0.8 x 0.8 inches) for print materials. For posters or displays viewed from a distance, increase the size proportionally.

Too Small

QR codes smaller than 2 cm may be difficult to scan in less-than-ideal conditions.

Good Size

2-3 cm for business cards, 8+ cm for posters, with proper quiet zone.

Maintain Good Contrast

High contrast between the QR code foreground and background is crucial for reliable scanning. The classic black on white offers the best readability, but dark colors on light backgrounds can also work well.

Poor Contrast

Yellow on white, light gray on white, or similar low-contrast combinations.

Good Contrast

Black on white, dark blue on white, or black on light yellow.

Include a Call to Action

Always include a clear call to action near your QR code. Tell users what they'll get by scanning it. For example, "Scan to view our menu," "Scan for exclusive discounts," or "Scan to connect to WiFi."

No Context

QR code with no explanation about what happens when scanned.

Clear Instructions

"Scan to download our app" with a logo or icon that reinforces the message.

Test Before Distributing

Always test your QR codes on multiple devices and in different lighting conditions before printing or publishing them. This ensures they work correctly and link to the right content.

Untested QR Codes

Publishing without testing can lead to broken links or poor scanning experience.

Thorough Testing

Test on multiple devices, in different lighting, and from printed samples if applicable.

Everything you need to know about our QR code generator

A QR (Quick Response) code is a two-dimensional barcode that can be scanned using a smartphone camera. When scanned, it quickly redirects to websites, displays text, adds contact information to your phone, connects to WiFi networks, and more. Unlike traditional barcodes that can only store information horizontally, QR codes store data both horizontally and vertically, allowing them to contain much more information in a smaller space.

Error correction allows QR codes to be readable even if they're partially damaged or obscured. We offer four error correction levels:

  • Low (L): 7% of the code can be restored if damaged
  • Medium (M): 15% of the code can be restored if damaged (recommended for most uses)
  • Quartile (Q): 25% of the code can be restored if damaged
  • High (H): 30% of the code can be restored if damaged

Higher error correction makes your QR code more resilient, but also makes the code more complex and slightly larger. For marketing materials that might get dirty or partially obscured, we recommend Quartile (Q) or High (H) correction levels.

Shortening your URL before creating a QR code offers several benefits:

  1. Simpler QR Code: Long URLs create more complex QR codes that can be harder to scan, especially when printed small or in low lighting conditions.
  2. Faster Scanning: Simpler QR codes with fewer data points can be scanned more quickly and reliably.
  3. Better Printing: Simpler QR codes print more clearly on various materials and at smaller sizes.
  4. Analytics: When you use TinyURL.run's URL shortener, you also get access to click analytics for your QR code, letting you track scans and user behavior.

For the best results, we recommend checking the "Shorten URL with TinyURL.run first" option when creating QR codes for long website addresses.

Most modern smartphones can scan QR codes directly through their built-in camera apps:

For iPhone (iOS 11 or later):

  1. Open the Camera app
  2. Point the camera at the QR code
  3. A notification will appear when the code is recognized
  4. Tap the notification to open the encoded content

For Android phones:

  1. Open the Camera app
  2. If your phone supports it, point the camera at the QR code
  3. Tap the notification that appears
  4. If your camera doesn't automatically scan QR codes, you may need to enable Google Lens or download a QR code scanner app from the Google Play Store

For older phones that don't have built-in QR code scanning, there are many free QR code scanner apps available in the App Store or Google Play Store.
